## 1) Fanny

Fanny is the clearest blue-buff snowball assassin on this list. Officially, her cable damage scales with flying speed and her hits apply **Prey Mark**, so every build decision should either increase her first-catch damage or keep her alive long enough to take a second rotation. She is a **Jungle Assassin** and her mechanical ceiling is extreme. ([Mobile Legends][2])

**Mechanics ratings**
Damage 9 | Burst 10 | DPS 7 | Mobility 10 | Sustain 3 | Durability 4 | CC 1 | Teamfight 6 | Objective 7 | Split Push 8 | Carry 10 | Difficulty 10

**Hero analysis**
Role & best position: Jungle only.
Lane priority: blue buff, then level 4 tempo.
Win condition: get one early kill, convert it into Turtle control, then keep the map hostage.
Strengths: fastest access, strongest pick pressure, best shutdown on immobile backlines.
Weaknesses: anti-cable hard stops, energy dependence, high shutdown risk.
Power spikes: level 4, first damage item, blue buff control, 2-item dive window.
Scaling: Early 9 / Mid 10 / Late 7.
Passive & skill synergy: faster flight means more damage and smoother Prey Mark conversion; movement speed and penetration are worth more than raw crit on her.

**Builds**

1. **Core** — Tough Boots > Blade of the Heptaseas > Malefic Roar > Hunter Strike > Blade of Despair > Immortality.
   Heptaseas gives the best first-hit punish, Malefic Roar stops armor from deleting your cable combo, Hunter Strike fixes repositioning, BoD closes kills, and Immortality buys a second dive.
   Ratings: Damage 9, Survivability 6, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 10.

2. **Max Damage** — Tough Boots > Blade of the Heptaseas > Sky Piercer > Malefic Roar > Blade of Despair > Hunter Strike.
   This is the purest “one target disappears” setup; Sky Piercer makes your already-fatal entries even harder to survive.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 3, Teamfight 5, Consistency 5, Carry 10.

3. **Solo Queue** — Tough Boots > Blade of the Heptaseas > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar > Queen’s Wings > Immortality.
   Better than glass cannon when teammates do not draft for you; Queen’s Wings converts failed dives into survival.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 8, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 9.

4. **Competitive** — Tough Boots > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar > Queen’s Wings > Immortality > Blade of Despair.
   Safer than greedy burst because coordinated teams punish shutdowns and force longer fights.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 9, Teamfight 8, Consistency 9, Carry 8.

5. **Anti-Tank** — Tough Boots > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar > Sea Halberd > Blade of Despair > Immortality.
   Sea Halberd matters when healing or giant HP pools are the real problem; it solves “I can reach them but cannot finish.”
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 6, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

6. **Anti-Burst** — Tough Boots > Athena’s Shield > Queen’s Wings > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar > Immortality.
   Athena’s Shield is the best answer to getting erased before the cable reset.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 10, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

7. **Anti-CC** — Tough Boots > Brute Force Breastplate > Queen’s Wings > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality > Malefic Roar.
   This is not literal CC immunity, but it gives enough movement and layered bulk to survive the first lockdown window.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 9, Teamfight 7, Consistency 7, Carry 7.

8. **Snowball** — Tough Boots > Blade of the Heptaseas > Sky Piercer > Hunter Strike > Blade of Despair > Malefic Roar.
   Best when you are already ahead and can protect stacks by ending fights instantly.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 4, Teamfight 6, Consistency 6, Carry 10.

9. **Late Game** — Tough Boots > Malefic Roar > Hunter Strike > Blade of Despair > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality.
   Late Fanny needs one defensive bridge and maximum pen; pure greed gets punished by one clean CC chain.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 7, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

**Emblems**

1. **Assassin — Rupture / Master Assassin / Killing Spree**. Best overall.
2. **Fighter — Firmness / Festival of Blood / Brave Smite**. Best defensive bruiser path.
3. **Assassin — Rupture / Master Assassin / Lethal Ignition**. Best snowball burst path.

Best Overall Emblem: Assassin.
Aggressive Emblem: Assassin with Lethal Ignition.
Defensive Emblem: Fighter with Festival of Blood and Brave Smite.
Scaling Emblem: Fighter bruiser setup.

**Battle spells**

1. **Retribution (Ice)** — mandatory for jungle tempo and blue control.
2. **Flicker** — only for niche angle correction or off-role play.
3. **Purify** — situational into layered CC if you are forced into a riskier draft.

**Matchups**
Strong against immobile mages/marksmen and split backlines.
Weak against grouped peel and any draft that can force her off flight.
Hard counters: Franco, Khufra, Saber, Akai, Chou, Minsitthar.
Best allies: Diggie, Mathilda, Atlas, Tigreal, Angela.
Ban priorities: Franco, Khufra, Saber, Akai.

**Advanced notes**
Hidden synergy: Heptaseas is stronger than it looks because Fanny almost always lands the first meaningful hit in a fight.
Common mistake: building pure damage and donating shutdown gold.
Common gameplay mistake: forcing a cable angle without blue buff or without checking enemy CC.
Patch-specific edge: current battlefield pressure still rewards early snowball more than slow scaling, which is exactly what Fanny wants.

**Meta verification**
The common Fanny burst build is close to optimal, but the public tendency to go full greed too early is wrong. One defensive slot earlier usually gives more wins than one extra damage slot.

**Final verdict**
Best Build: Core
Best Emblem: Assassin
Best Talent Setup: Rupture / Master Assassin / Killing Spree
Best Spell: Retribution
Current Tier: top-end but draft-sensitive
Carry Potential: 10/10
Difficulty: 10/10
Overall Power: 9/10

## 2) Ling

Ling is a conditional jungle carry, not a blind pick. His wall mobility and Lightness-point economy define his entire value, and the official hero page says he gains Lightness Points while on walls; current patch logic still rewards safe blue cycles and clean backline access more than raw stat-checking. ([Mobile Legends][3])

**Mechanics ratings**
Damage 8 | Burst 8 | DPS 8 | Mobility 10 | Sustain 4 | Durability 5 | CC 2 | Teamfight 7 | Objective 7 | Split Push 9 | Carry 10 | Difficulty 9

**Hero analysis**
Role & best position: Jungle.
Lane priority: blue buff and wall access first, fights second.
Win condition: pick off the backline before the enemy front line can collapse.
Strengths: unmatched terrain access, excellent cleanup, strong map pressure.
Weaknesses: blue-buff dependence, anti-dash counters, weak blind-pick reliability.
Power spikes: first crit item, 2-item reset, walls plus ult.
Scaling: Early 7 / Mid 10 / Late 8.
Passive & skill synergy: crit and skill-to-basic cadence work best when he can hop walls continuously.

**Builds**

1. **Core** — Tough Boots > Berserker’s Fury > Haas’s Claws > Endless Battle > Blade of Despair > Malefic Roar.
   This is the cleanest crit-reset profile: crit for burst, Haas for sustain, Endless for skill cadence, BoD for finish, Malefic for armor.
   Ratings: Damage 9, Survivability 6, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 10.

2. **Max Damage** — Magic Boots > Sky Piercer > Berserker’s Fury > Haas’s Claws > Blade of Despair > Malefic Roar.
   Highest execute pressure when the enemy backline is already exposed.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 3, Teamfight 5, Consistency 5, Carry 10.

3. **Solo Queue** — Tough Boots > Berserker’s Fury > Haas’s Claws > Endless Battle > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality.
   Better than glass cannon because solo queue throws random CC and bad engages at you all game.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 8, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 9.

4. **Competitive** — Tough Boots > Berserker’s Fury > Endless Battle > Malefic Roar > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality.
   More stable in coordinated drafts where one bad dive loses the game.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 9, Teamfight 8, Consistency 9, Carry 8.

5. **Anti-Tank** — Tough Boots > Berserker’s Fury > Endless Battle > Malefic Roar > Sea Halberd > Blade of Despair.
   Sea Halberd is the fix when healing and high HP are the real walls in front of you.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 6, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

6. **Anti-Burst** — Tough Boots > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality > Berserker’s Fury > Endless Battle > Malefic Roar.
   The shield plus revive are worth more than one greedy slot when burst is the real threat.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 10, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

7. **Anti-CC** — Tough Boots > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality > Queen’s Wings > Berserker’s Fury > Malefic Roar.
   This is survival through the first lockdown cycle, not true CC immunity.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 9, Teamfight 7, Consistency 7, Carry 7.

8. **Snowball** — Magic Boots > Sky Piercer > Berserker’s Fury > Haas’s Claws > Endless Battle > Blade of Despair.
   When you can keep stacks and finish games early, this is the most punishing setup.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 4, Teamfight 6, Consistency 6, Carry 10.

9. **Late Game** — Tough Boots > Berserker’s Fury > Endless Battle > Malefic Roar > Blade of Despair > Immortality.
   You still need one revive slot in long games because one bad rotation ends Ling’s value.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 7, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

**Emblems**

1. **Assassin — Fatal / Seasoned Hunter / War Cry**. Best overall.
2. **Assassin — Fatal / Seasoned Hunter / Killing Spree**. Best aggressive setup.
3. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / War Cry**. Best scaling versus armor.

Best Overall Emblem: Assassin with Fatal and War Cry.
Aggressive Emblem: Assassin with Killing Spree.
Defensive Emblem: Assassin with Rupture and Killing Spree.
Scaling Emblem: Assassin with Rupture and War Cry.

**Battle spells**

1. **Retribution (Ice)** — best jungle spell.
2. **Flame Retribution** — better when you are already ahead and need kill pressure.
3. **Flicker** — situational only, usually for off-meta lane play.

**Matchups**
Strong against immobile backlines and comps that cannot stop wall access.
Weak against hard anti-dash and point-click lockdown.
Hard counters: Khufra, Franco, Saber, Kaja, Minsitthar, Valir.
Best allies: Diggie, Mathilda, Angela, Atlas.
Ban priorities: Khufra, Franco, Saber, Kaja, Minsitthar.

**Advanced notes**
Hidden synergy: crit Ling is not about raw auto-attacks; it is about converting every reset into a lethal exit.
Common mistake: fighting before your walls and blue path are ready.
Common gameplay mistake: ulting too early and wasting your wall exit.
Patch-specific edge: current draft tempo still rewards heroes who can collapse side lanes before full 5v5s begin.

**Meta verification**
The standard crit reset build is still the baseline, but I rate it as “best only when the draft lets it breathe.” When the enemy stacks armor or healing, the anti-armor branch wins more games.

**Final verdict**
Best Build: Core
Best Emblem: Assassin
Best Talent Setup: Fatal / Seasoned Hunter / War Cry
Best Spell: Retribution (Ice)
Current Tier: high but conditional
Carry Potential: 10/10
Difficulty: 9/10
Overall Power: 9/10

## 3) Hayabusa

Hayabusa is an isolation assassin. His Shadow Mark stacking rewards single-target pressure, and the official hero page notes that each stack increases damage to the marked enemy, which is why his best builds are all about first-hit access plus clean exit. ([Mobile Legends][4])

**Mechanics ratings**
Damage 8 | Burst 9 | DPS 7 | Mobility 9 | Sustain 4 | Durability 4 | CC 1 | Teamfight 5 | Objective 7 | Split Push 10 | Carry 9 | Difficulty 8

**Hero analysis**
Role & best position: Jungle.
Lane priority: pick-off angles, not brute-force skirmishes.
Win condition: isolate a carry, kill them, and leave before the enemy frontline can react.
Strengths: extreme side-lane threat, excellent cleanup, strong escape routes.
Weaknesses: group fights, hard CC, point-and-click burst.
Power spikes: level 4, first mobility item, first execute item.
Scaling: Early 8 / Mid 10 / Late 7.
Passive & skill synergy: Shadow Mark makes long enough mark-up windows matter; you want one target, not a cluster.

**Builds**

1. **Core** — Magic Boots > Hunter Strike > Blade of the Heptaseas > Sky Piercer > Malefic Roar > Immortality.
   Hunter Strike fixes movement and cycle speed, Heptaseas sharpens the first hit, Sky Piercer converts isolated targets into guaranteed kills.
   Ratings: Damage 9, Survivability 6, Teamfight 6, Consistency 8, Carry 9.

2. **Max Damage** — Swift Boots > Sky Piercer > Blade of the Heptaseas > Hunter Strike > Blade of Despair > Malefic Roar.
   Pure backline deletion when the enemy is already split.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 3, Teamfight 5, Consistency 5, Carry 10.

3. **Solo Queue** — Tough Boots > Hunter Strike > Blade of the Heptaseas > Malefic Roar > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality.
   More forgiving because solo queue throws random hard CC and bad collapse timings at you.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 8, Teamfight 6, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

4. **Competitive** — Tough Boots > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality > Sea Halberd.
   Better when fights are coordinated and you need to stay alive after the first kill.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 9, Teamfight 7, Consistency 9, Carry 8.

5. **Anti-Tank** — Tough Boots > Hunter Strike > War Axe > Malefic Roar > Sea Halberd > Immortality.
   War Axe is the “I need longer fighting power” answer when one target will not die in one ult.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 7, Teamfight 6, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

6. **Anti-Burst** — Tough Boots > Rose Gold Meteor > Queen’s Wings > Malefic Roar > Immortality > Hunter Strike.
   These slots exist to keep you alive through the counterburst after Shadow Kill ends.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 10, Teamfight 6, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

7. **Anti-CC** — Tough Boots > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality > Queen’s Wings > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar.
   This is the “survive the first lockdown and still move again” version.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 9, Teamfight 6, Consistency 8, Carry 7.

8. **Snowball** — Swift Boots > Sky Piercer > Blade of the Heptaseas > Hunter Strike > Blade of Despair > Malefic Roar.
   Best when you are already chaining picks and want the game closed fast.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 4, Teamfight 6, Consistency 6, Carry 10.

9. **Late Game** — Tough Boots > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar > Blade of Despair > Immortality > Rose Gold Meteor.
   Late games punish greed; this keeps one defensive bridge while preserving kill pressure.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 7, Teamfight 6, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

**Emblems**

1. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / Killing Spree**. Best overall.
2. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / Lethal Ignition**. Best aggressive setup.
3. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / War Cry**. Best scaling setup.

Best Overall Emblem: Assassin with Killing Spree.
Aggressive Emblem: Assassin with Lethal Ignition.
Defensive Emblem: Assassin with Killing Spree and defensive items.
Scaling Emblem: Assassin with War Cry.

**Battle spells**

1. **Retribution (Ice)** — best jungle spell because it keeps the target in ult range.
2. **Flame Retribution** — better for snowball and finish windows.
3. **Flicker** — niche only, usually off-role.

**Matchups**
Strong against immobile backlines and single-target squishies.
Weak against hard point-click CC and large persistent AoE zones.
Hard counters: Kaja, Franco, Saber, Phoveus, Cyclops, Valir.
Best allies: Atlas, Tigreal, Diggie, Mathilda, Angela.
Ban priorities: Kaja, Franco, Saber, Phoveus, Valir.

**Advanced notes**
Hidden synergy: Hunter Strike is more valuable than it looks because it restores the “exit” part of your combo.
Common mistake: ulting into a grouped team instead of a solo target.
Common gameplay mistake: wasting Shadow Kill on minion clutter or tank body blocks.
Patch-specific edge: split-heavy games still give Hayabusa the cleanest punish windows of the six heroes here.

**Meta verification**
The current public default of magic boots plus Hunter Strike and execute tools is already directionally correct. I prefer one more safety slot earlier if the enemy can force late fights.

**Final verdict**
Best Build: Core
Best Emblem: Assassin
Best Talent Setup: Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / Killing Spree
Best Spell: Retribution (Ice)
Current Tier: high, but draft-sensitive
Carry Potential: 9/10
Difficulty: 8/10
Overall Power: 8/10

## 4) Lancelot

Lancelot is an invincibility-frame assassin. His strength is not just mobility; it is the fact that he can phase through the enemy’s key response windows if you time the dashes correctly. Current patch logic still rewards that, but only if you avoid point-and-click control and overforce into AoE. ([MLBBHub][5])

**Mechanics ratings**
Damage 8 | Burst 9 | DPS 7 | Mobility 9 | Sustain 4 | Durability 4 | CC 2 | Teamfight 6 | Objective 6 | Split Push 8 | Carry 9 | Difficulty 8

**Hero analysis**
Role & best position: Jungle.
Lane priority: isolate a target, then reset.
Win condition: one clean backline delete, then a second dive if the fight continues.
Strengths: I-frame abuse, clean pick pressure, excellent skirmish tempo.
Weaknesses: suppress, persistent AoE, missing your combo window.
Power spikes: first CDR/tempo item, 2-item burst, snowball stacks.
Scaling: Early 7 / Mid 10 / Late 7.
Passive & skill synergy: Soul Cutter rewards chained movement; Puncture resets are the engine, not the bonus.

**Builds**

1. **Core** — Tough Boots > War Axe > Hunter Strike > Endless Battle > Blade of Despair > Immortality.
   War Axe and Hunter Strike keep the cadence alive, Endless Battle cashes the skill-to-hit window, BoD finishes the target, Immortality gives a second entry.
   Ratings: Damage 9, Survivability 6, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 9.

2. **Max Damage** — Magic Boots > Sky Piercer > Blade of Heptaseas > Hunter Strike > Blade of Despair > Malefic Roar.
   This is the purest “one target should die immediately” path.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 3, Teamfight 5, Consistency 5, Carry 10.

3. **Solo Queue** — Tough Boots > War Axe > Hunter Strike > Rose Gold Meteor > Blade of Despair > Immortality.
   Safer when solo queue chaos means you cannot trust perfect spacing.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 8, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 9.

4. **Competitive** — Tough Boots > War Axe > Hunter Strike > Endless Battle > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality.
   This is the highest-value teamfight version when the enemy can punish greedy dives.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 9, Teamfight 8, Consistency 9, Carry 8.

5. **Anti-Tank** — Tough Boots > War Axe > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar > Sea Halberd > Blade of Despair.
   This solves armor stacking and sustain, which are the two main reasons Lancelot’s kill window disappears.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 6, Teamfight 6, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

6. **Anti-Burst** — Tough Boots > Rose Gold Meteor > Queen’s Wings > War Axe > Hunter Strike > Immortality.
   Keeps you alive long enough to finish or reset when the enemy tries to trade burst for your entry.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 10, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

7. **Anti-CC** — Tough Boots > Rose Gold Meteor > Immortality > Queen’s Wings > War Axe > Hunter Strike.
   Not real CC immunity, but enough bulk to survive the first response and escape.
   Ratings: Damage 7, Survivability 9, Teamfight 7, Consistency 7, Carry 7.

8. **Snowball** — Magic Boots > Sky Piercer > Blade of Heptaseas > Hunter Strike > Malefic Roar > Blade of Despair.
   Best when you are already winning skirmishes and want the game to end immediately.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 4, Teamfight 6, Consistency 6, Carry 10.

9. **Late Game** — Tough Boots > War Axe > Hunter Strike > Endless Battle > Malefic Roar > Immortality.
   Late-game Lancelot wants survivability back in the build because one missed I-frame loses the fight.
   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 7, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

**Emblems**

1. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / Killing Spree**. Best overall.
2. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / Lethal Ignition**. Best aggressive setup.
3. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / War Cry**. Best scaling setup.

Best Overall Emblem: Assassin with Killing Spree.
Aggressive Emblem: Assassin with Lethal Ignition.
Defensive Emblem: Assassin with Killing Spree plus Rose Gold / Immortality.
Scaling Emblem: Assassin with War Cry.

**Battle spells**

1. **Retribution (Ice)** — best jungle spell for sticking a target in range.
2. **Flame Retribution** — best when snowballing and you already have the lead.
3. **Flicker** — situational off-role/angle-fix option.

**Matchups**
Strong against skill-shot-heavy drafts and immobile backlines.
Weak against suppress, point-and-click CC, and heavy persistent AoE.
Hard counters: Kaja, Franco, Saber, Phoveus, Cyclops, Valir.
Best allies: Atlas, Tigreal, Mathilda, Angela, Diggie.
Ban priorities: Kaja, Franco, Saber, Phoveus, Cyclops.

**Advanced notes**
Hidden synergy: Endless Battle fits him better than it looks because his combo repeatedly creates skill-to-basic windows.
Common mistake: diving before the enemy spends their key CC.
Common gameplay mistake: trying to teamfight front-to-back instead of using one clean flank.
Patch-specific edge: current drafts still overvalue static backline mages, which Lancelot can punish hard if you read the entry timing.

**Meta verification**
The current public jungle build is close to correct. The stronger version is the one that respects how often coordinated teams can punish the first dive.

**Final verdict**
Best Build: Core
Best Emblem: Assassin
Best Talent Setup: Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / Killing Spree
Best Spell: Retribution (Ice)
Current Tier: high-skill niche
Carry Potential: 9/10
Difficulty: 8/10
Overall Power: 8/10

## 5) Helcurt

Helcurt is a silence assassin that happens to burst. His kit is about stealing the enemy’s reaction window; his darkness ultimate and silence effects are what make him useful, and his build should either amplify the first execute or let him pivot into longer stinger fights against tanks. ([MLBBHub][6])

**Mechanics ratings**
Damage 8 | Burst 8 | DPS 7 | Mobility 7 | Sustain 4 | Durability 4 | CC 6 | Teamfight 7 | Objective 6 | Split Push 6 | Carry 8 | Difficulty 7

**Hero analysis**
Role & best position: Jungle, with Roam as a real secondary route.
Lane priority: pick-off windows and vision denial.
Win condition: make the enemy carry lose visibility, then delete them before the fight becomes readable.
Strengths: silence, fog pressure, ambush threat, strong into low-peel drafts.
Weaknesses: hard lockdown, short-range entry, falling off into dense frontlines.
Power spikes: first damage item, level 4 darkness, 2-item kill window.
Scaling: Early 8 / Mid 9 / Late 6.
Passive & skill synergy: stinger stacking makes attack tempo matter more than raw burst alone; after the silence opens a window, he still needs the right follow-up.

   Ratings: Damage 8, Survivability 6, Teamfight 7, Consistency 8, Carry 8.

   The pure execution version for squishy backlines.
   Ratings: Damage 10, Survivability 3, Teamfight 5, Consistency 5, Carry 10.

**Emblems**

1. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / Killing Spree**. Best overall.
2. **Assassin — Rupture / Master Assassin / Lethal Ignition**. Best aggressive burst setup.
3. **Assassin — Rupture / Seasoned Hunter / War Cry**. Best scaling or anti-frontline setup.

## Meta verification

The common meta instinct on these six is either “full tank” or “full utility.” That is only half-right. **Diggie, Floryn, and Marcel** want CDR plus one survival bridge, **Kaja** wants utility bruiser stats, **Uranus and Gloo** want regeneration plus resistances, and none of them benefit much from greedy damage unless you are intentionally drafting a cheese lane. That is the stronger alternative to the popular one-size-fits-all support builds. ([mobilelegends.com][2])
